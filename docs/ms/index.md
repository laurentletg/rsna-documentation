# Multiple Sclerosis Lesion Imaging

An introduction to neuroimaging anatomy and MS lesion imaging for new lab members.

---

## What is MS?

Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a chronic inflammatory demyelinating disease of the central nervous system (CNS). MRI is the cornerstone of diagnosis, monitoring, and treatment evaluation. Lesions appear as focal areas of demyelination and axonal loss, most visible on **T2-weighted** and **FLAIR** sequences.

---

## MRI Sequences and What They Show

| Sequence | Appearance of MS Lesions | Key Use |
|---|---|---|
| **T2-w** | Hyperintense (bright) | Detect all lesions |
| **FLAIR** | Hyperintense (bright); CSF suppressed | Best for periventricular and cortical lesions |
| **T1-w (post-Gd)** | Enhancing = active/acute lesion | Inflammation, blood-brain barrier breakdown |
| **T1-w (pre-Gd)** | Hypointense "black holes" | Chronic axonal loss |
| **DWI** | Variable; restricted diffusion in acute phase | Differentiate from acute stroke |

## MS Lesion Locations

MS lesions follow characteristic topographic patterns. The McDonald criteria use **four locations** for dissemination in space (DIS):

| Location | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Periventricular** | Along lateral ventricles; "Dawson's fingers" perpendicular to ventricles on sagittal FLAIR |
| **Juxtacortical/cortical** | Touching the cortex; may be cortical or leuco-cortical |
| **Infratentorial** | Brainstem and cerebellum |
| **Spinal cord** | Cervical > thoracic; typically peripheral and < 2 vertebral segments |
| **Optic nerve** *(2024 criteria)* | Fifth site added in 2024 revision |

!!! note "Dawson's Fingers"
    Periventricular lesions oriented perpendicular to the lateral ventricles, following medullary veins. A hallmark of MS.
    [Radiopaedia — Dawson's Fingers](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/dawson-fingers)

## McDonald Diagnostic Criteria (2024)

The McDonald criteria define MS diagnosis using **dissemination in space (DIS)** and **dissemination in time (DIT)**.

The **2024 revision** (Lancet Neurology) introduced:

- Optic nerve as a **5th topographic DIS site**
- CSF biomarker (kappa free light chains) as a substitute for DIT
- **Central vein sign (CVS)** and **paramagnetic rim lesions (PRL)** as optional high-specificity markers
